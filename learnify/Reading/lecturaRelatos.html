• THE GREAT BEAR IN THE SKY

    In the past, when people were lost in the woods, they often looked for a group of stars called the Big Dipper. This told them which direction was north. The Big Dipper is also known as Ursa Major, which means “Great Bear” in Latin. Why? The stars form a shape similar to a big bear. One myth about Ursa Major comes from a Native American tribe in Canada. The story says there was an enormous bear that was very vicious. His favorite pastime was attacking villages and people. Finally, the best warrior were sent to hunt the bear. When the bear realized they were hunting him, he was so terrified that he ran away. The warriors chased him for months and months, and he started to get tired. He ran into the sky to escape the warriors, but he did not expect them to pursue him! They entered the sky with him and chased him around Ursa Major, continuing to circle around it. One day in fall, one of the warriors shot the bear with an arrow. He did not die, but he began to bleed. So every fall, when the bear approaches the horizon, his blood drips onto the Earth. This causes the trees to turn red and oranges.
