• MONET’S GARDEN

    Monet’s early paintings were in the realist style, but later he painted in his own impressionist style. He started to focus on light and color, not people or objects. One of Monet’s most famous subjects is water lilies. He painted over 250 water lily paintings. Many of them look alike, but no two are exactly the same. He painted them in various colors and types of light. Finally, he was able to capture the way the light changed their appearance. In these paintings, the flowers lose significance and the mood becomes an important feature. As Monet focused on emotion rather than realism, people soon began to see how light can be beautiful. Thanks to Monet, modern painters have the freedom to paint what they feel, not just what they see.
